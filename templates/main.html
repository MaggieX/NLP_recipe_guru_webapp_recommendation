How do we recommend personalized recipes

"Recipe Guru" uses NLP technologies to recommend similar recipes to the ones one might like, currently based on 18000 recipes out of Recipe 1M+. Under the hood, this web app uses Recipe 1M+ data and natural language processing (NLP) models to recommend similar recipes. It uses similarity matrix to look for similarities between recipes based on ingredients and instructions. Bag of Words (BOW), Doc2Vec and BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers) models are used to estimate degrees of similarities between recipes, and provide recipe recommendations. Here is a blog post I wrote that talks about BERT and other recent NLP technologies, including XLNet. To read more about the details of the implementation of this project, you can read more here. Go to the "Explore" section for data science related visualization of the data: 1) Interactive plot with Principal Component Analysis results for dimensionality reduction based on Bag of words embedding (powered by Altair).

About Me

Hi! I am Maggie, a PhD student based in LA. Probably like you, I love exploring new food. During shutdown, my friends and I are searching for cooking ideas that can be turned into our next meal. This was the motivation of creating this recipe recommendation website -- to make your next meal planning experience more like a serendipity.
